What is composite bonding?

A tooth-coloured resin material that's sculpted and bonded directly to your teeth to restore their shape, size, and appearance.

Perfect for treating:

  • Chipped or broken teeth

    Restore the original shape and function

  • Small gaps between teeth

    Close spaces for a more even smile

  • Discoloured teeth

    Cover stains and improve colour

  • Worn or short teeth

    Build up length and restore proportion

  • Misshapen teeth

    Improve the shape and contour

Why choose bonding?

  • • Quick treatment - usually completed in one visit
  • • Conservative - minimal tooth removal required
  • • Affordable alternative to veneers
  • • Natural appearance when expertly applied
  • • Reversible treatment option
  • • Can be repaired if damaged

The bonding process

A careful, artistic process that restores your teeth while maintaining their natural function and appearance.

1

Assessment & Planning (£95)

We examine the teeth to be treated and discuss your goals. Bonding isn't suitable for all situations - we'll advise if veneers or other treatments would be more appropriate.

  • Tooth condition assessment
  • Colour matching
  • Treatment planning

Comprehensive Assessment

2

Tooth Preparation

We gently roughen the tooth surface to help the bonding material adhere properly. This process requires no anaesthetic and removes very little natural tooth structure.

"I was amazed how gentle the process was. Dr. Jandoo explained every step and I felt completely comfortable." — Emma L.

Gentle Preparation

3

Artistic Application

The composite resin is carefully applied in layers, sculpted to the desired shape, and hardened with a special light. Each layer is precisely shaped to recreate natural tooth anatomy.

Treatment time:

  • • Single tooth: 30-60 minutes
  • • Multiple teeth: 1-2 hours
  • • Usually completed in one visit
  • • No temporary restorations needed

Bonding vs veneers: Which is right for you?

Our £95 consultation helps determine the best treatment for your specific situation and goals.

Choose Bonding If:

  • • You have minor chips or gaps
  • • You want a conservative approach
  • • Budget is a consideration
  • • You want treatment completed quickly
  • • The damage is limited to one or two teeth
  • • You prefer a reversible treatment
