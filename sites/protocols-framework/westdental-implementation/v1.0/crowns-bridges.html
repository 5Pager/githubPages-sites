Crowns or bridges: Which do you need?

Both treatments restore your smile's function and appearance, but they serve different purposes.

Dental Crowns

A crown is like a cap that completely covers a damaged tooth, restoring its shape, size, strength, and appearance.

Perfect for:

  • • Large cavities or broken teeth
  • • Teeth weakened by root canal treatment
  • • Severely worn or cracked teeth
  • • Misshapen or discoloured teeth
  • • Protecting a tooth after major dental work

Crown materials:

  • • Porcelain - most natural appearance
  • • Zirconia - strongest, most durable
  • • Porcelain-fused-to-metal - traditional strength

From £695

per crown

Treatment process:

  • • Consultation and planning
  • • Tooth preparation and impressions
  • • Temporary crown fitted
  • • Custom crown created (7-10 days)
  • • Permanent crown fitted
  • • Usually requires 2 appointments

Dental Bridges

A bridge replaces missing teeth by anchoring artificial teeth to the natural teeth on either side of the gap.

Perfect for:

  • • Replacing 1-3 missing teeth
  • • When adjacent teeth also need crowns
  • • Preventing remaining teeth from shifting
  • • Restoring chewing and speaking ability
  • • Alternative to dental implants

Bridge types:

  • • Traditional - most common and durable
  • • Cantilever - when only one adjacent tooth
  • • Maryland - minimal preparation required

From £1,650

3-unit bridge

Treatment process:

  • • Consultation and planning
  • • Adjacent teeth prepared
  • • Impressions taken
  • • Temporary bridge fitted
  • • Custom bridge created (10-14 days)
  • • Permanent bridge fitted

Caring for your restoration

With proper care, crowns and bridges can last 15-20 years or more.

Daily care:

  • • Brush twice daily with fluoride toothpaste
  • • Floss daily (especially important with bridges)
  • • Use bridge floss or water flosser if needed
  • • Rinse with antibacterial mouthwash
  • • Avoid chewing ice or hard objects

Professional care

Regular check-ups and cleanings help ensure your restoration stays healthy and functional.

Watch for:

  • • Pain or sensitivity when biting
  • • Loose or missing restoration
  • • Swelling around the treated area
  • • Dark lines at the gum line
  • • Bad taste or odour
