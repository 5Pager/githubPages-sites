This wasn't the plan.

From NHS burnout to creating safe harbors for dental transformation. The story of how one dentist discovered that helping people feel safe matters more than following industry standards.

[Dr. Karm Jandoo Portrait]

The Journey to Authentic Care

1
[NHS Training Image]

The Conventional Path

Like most dentists, I started with NHS training, believing that technical excellence was all that mattered. Rush through appointments, hit targets, follow protocols. The system worked—for the system.

But I kept noticing something the metrics didn't measure: the fear in people's eyes, the shame they carried about their teeth, the way they apologized for existing.

"I realized I was treating teeth, not people."
2
[Private Practice Transition]

The Awakening Moment

Moving to private practice, I thought I'd found freedom. Better equipment, more time, higher quality work. But the pressure to sell, upsell, and compete with chains created a different kind of exhaustion.

Then came the moment that changed everything: a patient started crying in the chair— not from pain, but from relief. "No one's ever explained things to me like this," she said. "I finally feel safe."

"That's when I understood: my real skill wasn't in my hands—it was in creating calm."
3
[West Dental Vision]

Building the Safe Harbor

West Dental isn't about competing with chains or following industry trends. It's about creating a space where people can breathe, think, and make decisions without pressure or judgment.

Every detail—from the extra time in appointments to the way we explain treatments— is designed around one principle: helping people rediscover their authentic selves through their smile.

"Now when the mirror goes up, patients don't just see better teeth—they recognize themselves."
